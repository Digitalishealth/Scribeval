## Transcript

**GP:** What has brought you in today?

**Patient:** I've had a cough for six days. It started with a sore throat. I want antibiotics because I get asthma.

**GP:** Any fever, chest pain, shortness of breath, blood in the sputum, or worsening wheeze?

**Patient:** No fever or chest pain. I am a bit tight at night but my reliever helps. No blood.

**GP:** Your oxygen level is 98%, chest is clear, and your peak flow is close to your usual. This sounds viral. We will avoid antibiotics today.

**Patient:** Okay, but what if it gets worse?

**GP:** Use your asthma plan, salbutamol as needed, fluids, and come back urgently for breathlessness, fever over 38.5, chest pain, blue lips, coughing blood, or needing reliever more than every four hours. Review in 48 hours if not improving.
