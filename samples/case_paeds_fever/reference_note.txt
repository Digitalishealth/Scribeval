Consultation Note — Paediatric Review
Patient: Tommy, age 3.5 years, ~15kg
Attended with: Mother (Jess)

History of presenting complaint:
Unwell since Saturday with fever. Temperatures up to 40.2°C, typically 39–39.5°C, partially responsive to paracetamol and ibuprofen. Associated mild coryza and cough. Ear-pulling on the right since yesterday. Reduced oral intake but continuing to drink. 4–5 wet nappies per day. No vomiting, no diarrhoea, no rash. Alert and interactive between fever spikes, floppy and clingy during.

No neck stiffness, photophobia, or non-blanching rash.
Immunisations up-to-date.
Daycare exposure, gastro circulating but Tommy asymptomatic for GI.
No allergies. No regular medications.

Examination:
Tonly alert, interactive, no respiratory distress.
T 38.6°C (post-paracetamol), HR 135, RR 28, SpO2 98% RA. CRT <2s.
Skin: no rash, no petechiae.
ENT: Right TM erythematous, bulging, loss of light reflex. Left TM normal. Throat mildly erythematous, no exudate.
Chest clear bilaterally, no added sounds.
Abdomen soft, non-tender, no organomegaly.
Neck supple.

Assessment:
Acute right otitis media with systemic illness in a 3-year-old. Hydration maintained. No red-flag features for serious bacterial infection, meningococcal disease, or lower respiratory tract infection.

Plan:
1. Amoxicillin 500 mg PO TDS for 5 days (no penicillin allergy). Advised to stop and call immediately if rash develops.
2. Paracetamol 15 mg/kg/dose (225 mg) PO up to QID PRN for comfort. Alternate with ibuprofen if needed, do not co-administer. Treat comfort, not the numbers.
3. Maintain hydration. Encourage small, frequent fluids.
4. Safety-netting: return immediately / go to ED if any of:
   - Drowsiness, difficulty rousing, limpness
   - Poor oral intake, reduced wet nappies
   - Non-blanching rash
   - Respiratory distress, fast breathing
   - Neck stiffness, photophobia
5. Return if fever persists >48 hours after starting antibiotic, or if deteriorating.
6. Routine review in 1 week to confirm resolution.
7. Discussed signs of tympanic membrane perforation (discharge, relief of pain) — keep ear dry, no instrumentation, review if occurs.
