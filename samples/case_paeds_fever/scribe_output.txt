Consultation Note

Patient: Tommy (3 years)
Presenting complaint: Fever

History:
Parent reports Tommy has been unwell since Saturday with high fevers up to 40 degrees, treated at home with paracetamol and ibuprofen with partial response. Associated symptoms include runny nose, mild cough, and pulling at his right ear. He is eating less than usual but drinking and passing urine normally. No vomiting, no rash. He is well between fever spikes and plays when the fever comes down.

Immunisations up to date. Daycare attendance with sick contacts.

Examination:
Temperature 38.6. Heart rate 135. Respiratory rate 28. SpO2 98%.
Alert and interactive. Capillary refill less than 2 seconds.
ENT: right tympanic membrane red and bulging with loss of light reflex, consistent with acute otitis media. Left ear normal. Throat mildly erythematous.
Chest clear bilaterally.
Abdomen soft and non-tender.

Assessment:
Acute otitis media, right ear.

Plan:
1. Amoxicillin 500mg TDS for 5 days.
2. Paracetamol and ibuprofen for comfort and fever.
3. Review if symptoms worsen.
